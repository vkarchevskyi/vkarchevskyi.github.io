Linus Torvalds

The most valuable person in open source

Here's a time line of Linus Torvalds's life:

  • 1969 — Born in Helsinki, Finland
  • 1988 — Get into the University of Helsinki
  • 1991 — The first Linux prototypes were publicly released
  • 1994 — Version 1.0 of Linux was released
  • 1996 — Get a master's degree in computer science from the NODES research group
  • 2005 — Start developing Git, version control software that later became widely used
  • 2011 — Start developing Subsurface, software for logging and planning scuba dives

There're Linus Torvalds's awards:

  • 1998 — EFF Pioneer Award
  • 1999 — Honorary Doctorate Stockholm University
  • 2000 — Honorary Doctorate Helsinki University
  • 2000 — The Lovelace Medal - British Computer Society
  • 2001 — The Takeda Award for Social/Economic Well-Being
  • 2005 — The Vollum Award from Reed College
  • 2008 — Hall of Fellows of the Computer History Museum
  • 2010 — C&C Prize by the NEC Corporation
  • 2012 — Millennium Technology Prize
  • 2012 — Internet Hall of Fame
  • 2014 — IEEE Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2018 — IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award

“Most good programmers do programming not because they expect to get paid or get adulation by the public, but because it is fun to program.”

― Linus Torvalds
